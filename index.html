Our Entertainment Practice

Music - Film - T.V. - Visual Art - Design - Photography - Fashion - Literature - Theater (not exhaustive)

The entertainment industry can be very informal, which could end up hurting both parties involved. Written agreements are the best way to protect yourself, whether you are just starting out or are more established. There are many agreements, which will become necessary on your journey. These agreements will need to be drafted, reviewed, red-lined, negotiated, and/or revised by an attorney.

  • Music - Management, band, publishing, recording, licensing, and distribution agreements. Band trademark filing and consultation.
  • Film - Distribution, licensing, actor, and financing agreements. Film trademark filings and consultation.
    See mention in VARIETY Magazine.
  • Visual Arts - Museum and gallery acquisition, licensing, and distribution agreements.
  • T.V., Design, Photography, Fashion, Literature, Theater - licensing, distribution, production, and option agreements.

Meet Our Team

Davina Amiri, Attorney

    DAmiri@DavinaAmiriLaw.com

Davina Amiri is a transactional attorney, focusing on Entertainment, Intellectual Property, Real Estate and Business matters since 2006. She negotiates agreements on behalf of artists such as musicians, film makers, entrepreneurs, and designers to name a few. Davina provides legal counsel to a vast breath of businesses such as talent agencies, management, production and technology companies or start-ups. Whether representing an individual or business, she gives sound copyright and trademark advice. Davina performs Real Property transactions for home buyers, sellers, and investors in New York and New Jersey. Her past legal experience includes being a consultant for various top tier "AMLAW 100" law firms in their corporate litigation practice and representing individuals in contested and non-contested divorce actions, as well as drafting marriage settlement agreements.

Davina is dedicated to providing pro bono legal services. She gives legal advice to artists through the Entertainment, Arts, and Sports Law section (EASL) of the New York State Bar Association and Volunteer Lawyers for the Arts. She is on the Board of the Iranian-American Bar Association of NY and is Chair of the PR committee. Additionally, she represents unaccompanied minors for asylum through the non-profit, Kids In Need of Defense.

Davina is admitted and in good standing to the New Jersey State Bar (2006) and New York State Bar (2007); the U.S. District Court for the District of New Jersey, as well as the U.S. District Court for the Eastern and Southern Districts of New York.

During her legal internship at the Shanghai law firm, Lehman, Lee & Xu, Davina authored the article, “A New Dawn for China Trade,” Shanghai Business Review, July/August 2004. Vol. 1; Issue 4.

Davina gives Copyright Law presentations in conjunction with The Center for Active Learning. Past events include, "Legal Advice for Creatives," (2012) and "It's Mine! - How To Own Your Creative Tech Property; A Practical Copyright Overview," (2013). In 2012, Davina negotiated the distribution deal for the film "Gut" as noted by VARIETY Magazine online.

In 2013, Davina founded MusicianRights.org, an organization which promotes fair and balanced contract standards for doing business in the music industry.

Silvia Gaviria, Attorney

SGaviria@DavinaAmiriLaw.com

Silvia Gaviria represents a wide range of clients. Silvia has worked as a consultant at some of New York’s top Corporate Law Firms assisting in matters such as corporate mergers, anti-trust cases, and foreign corrupt practice act violation investigations. She has formed business entities for clients such as corporations and LLCs. She has represented clients in divorce proceedings and drafted and negotiated pre-nuptial agreements for clients before marriage. Silvia has drafted and negotiated various types of contracts and agreements for entertainment clients and inventors including but not limited to production agreements, development agreements and management agreements. Silvia focuses most of her time and expertise in real estate where she helps clients from the inception through the closing of the real estate purchase process. She represents clients in all boroughs, Westchester and Long Island with the purchase of condos, cooperatives and single and multi-family dwellings.

Silvia Gaviria worked in the not-for-profit sector before entering the field of law. She has remained active in the public sector by volunteering for probono work throughout her legal career. She has represented low income women in New York City in divorce proceedings for the non-profit, Her Justice (formerly InMotion), she has also volunteered her legal services to KIND (Kids In Need of Defense) and helped unaccompanied minors requesting asylum.

Silvia obtained her Bachelor's Degree from John Jay College of Criminal Justice, where she majored in Forensic Psychology and graduated Magna Cum Laude and attended Hofstra University School of Law, where she graduated from in 2004. Silvia is admitted and in good standing to the New York State Bar and she is fluent in Spanish.
