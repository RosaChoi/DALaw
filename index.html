Meet Our Team

Davina Amiri

Davina is a transactional attorney, focusing on Entertainment, Intellectual Property, and Real Property matters. She negotiates agreements on behalf of musicians, film makers, writers, and photographers to name a few. Provided there is no conflict, Davina negotiates on behalf of entertainment businesses such as talent agencies, management and publishing companies. She provides legal consultation to non-entertainment based businesses including start-ups, where she drafts and negotiates contracts, as well as copyright and trademark matters. Additionally, she performs Real Property transactions.

Davina gives Copyright Law presentations in conjunction with The Center for Active Learning. Past events include, "Legal Advice for Creatives," in 2012 and "It's Mine! - How To Own Your Creative Tech Property; A Practical Copyright Overview," in 2013.

In 2012, Davina negotiated the distribution deal for the film "Gut" as noted by Variety Magazine online.

In 2013, Davina founded MusicianRights.org, an organization which promotes fair and balanced contract standards for doing business in the music industry.

Davina is dedicated to providing pro bono legal services. She gives legal advice to artists through the Entertainment, Arts, and Sports Law section (EASL) of the New York State Bar Association and Volunteer Lawyers for the Arts. She is on the Board of the Iranian-American Bar Association of NY and is Chair of the PR committee. Additionally, She represents unaccompanied minors for asylum through the non-profit, Kids In Need of Defense.
